DraggableGrid

This is an example of a DraggableGrid. It extends the GWT Grid to support draggable columns. The DraggableGrid only implements the logic to detect the begin, movement and end of a column drag. The default ColumnDragListener that is attached is responsible for creating the visual representation of the column drag event, such as the dimmed column and detached column and the actual movement of column data. If the default listener is not appropriate for your needs you can remove it with a call to removeDefaultListener(). The checkboxes allow you to play with three of the properties available to control the behavior of the DraggableGrid. The text area displays information about the events the DraggableGrid is producing. See the javadoc for the DraggableGrid for more detail.

Usage:

  1. Add the jaxzin-gwt-1.1.jar to your project's classpath.
  2. To the gwt.xml for your Widget, add the following: <inherits name='com.jaxzin.gwt.ui.DraggableGrid'/>
  3. Add a new DraggableGrid to your widget, or use it as a drop-in replacement for an existing Grid in your Widget.
